Disaster Survival Guide

Prepare, Respond, and Survive with Expert Guidance

Emergency Preparation Kit

Keep these essential items in an easily accessible location:

  • Water (1 gallon per person per day for at least 3 days)
  • Non-perishable food for at least 3 days
  • Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
  • Flashlight and extra batteries
  • First aid kit and essential medications
  • Whistle for signaling help
  • Dust mask or N95 respirator
  • Plastic sheeting and duct tape for sheltering
  • Moist towelettes and garbage bags for sanitation
  • Wrench or pliers to turn off utilities
  • Manual can opener
  • Local maps
  • Cell phone with chargers/power bank
  • Important documents in waterproof container
  • Cash and credit cards
  • Infant formula and diapers if needed
  • Pet food and water
  • Bedding or warm clothing

Evacuation Planning

Know Your Routes: Identify at least two evacuation routes from your home and community.
Family Communication Plan: Establish a meeting point outside your neighborhood and designate an out-of-state contact person.
Important Documents: Keep copies of insurance policies, identification, medical records in a waterproof container.
Vehicle Readiness: Keep your car at least half full of gas during hurricane/storm season.

Important Numbers to Know

Save these numbers in your phone AND write them down in case phone batteries die:
  • Emergency: 911
  • Local Red Cross chapter
  • Local Police Department (non-emergency)
  • Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222
  • Utility company (gas, electric, water)
  • Insurance agent
  • Doctor and dentist
  • Family members' contact numbers

General Safety Rules During Any Disaster

Do NOT: Return home until authorities say it's safe. Do not touch downed power lines or damaged utilities.
DO: Stay calm, follow instructions from authorities, help your neighbors if safe, document damage for insurance.
Stay Informed: Listen to local news and emergency alerts. Download emergency alert apps.

Earthquake Safety Guide

Earthquakes strike without warning. Knowing what to do in the first moments can save your life.

BEFORE AN EARTHQUAKE

Secure Heavy Items: Bolt down water heaters, bookshelves, and tall furniture to walls.
Identify Safe Spots: Use interior doorways, against interior walls, or under sturdy tables in each room.
Know Shutoff Valves: Learn how to shut off gas, water, and electricity at main switches.
Practice Drills: Regularly practice "Drop, Cover, Hold On" with family members.

DURING AN EARTHQUAKE

DROP, COVER, HOLD ON!
  • DROP to the ground immediately
  • COVER your head and neck with your hands and arms
  • HOLD ON to a sturdy object and protect yourself
  • Stay in position for 60 seconds or until shaking stops
  • If outdoors, move away from buildings, trees, and power lines
  • If driving, pull over safely and stay in your vehicle with seatbelt on
  • If in an elevator, press floor buttons to exit at next floor
  • Do NOT run outside - falling debris is dangerous

AFTER AN EARTHQUAKE

Check for Injuries: Provide first aid if needed and call 911 for serious injuries.
Check Home: Look for hazards like gas leaks, broken glass, and structural damage.
Communication: Use text messages; calls may not work. Contact your out-of-state person.
Aftershocks: Expect aftershocks and be prepared to Drop, Cover, Hold On again.
Avoid Damaged Areas: Do not enter buildings with visible damage until declared safe.

Flood Safety Guide

Floods are the most common natural disaster. Proper preparation and quick action can save lives.

BEFORE A FLOOD

Know Your Risk: Check if your home is in a flood zone and verify flood insurance requirements.
Prepare Your Home: Install check valves in plumbing, seal basement cracks, grade soil away from foundation.
Elevate Utilities: Move HVAC, water heater, and electrical panels above expected flood level.
Monitor Weather: Set up flood alerts on your phone and know local evacuation routes.

DURING A FLOOD WARNING

Act quickly - you may have only minutes!
  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Avoid driving or walking through flooded areas
  • Turn off natural gas at main valve if ordered to evacuate
  • Do not use elevators
  • Close windows and doors to delay water entry
  • Bring pets and livestock to higher ground
  • If trapped in rising water, move to the roof
  • Call 911 if you need rescue

AFTER A FLOOD

Water Contamination: Do not drink tap water unless authorities declare it safe.
Mold Prevention: Open windows for ventilation, remove wet materials, dry homes quickly.
Document Damage: Take photos/videos of all damage for insurance claims.
Protective Equipment: Wear gloves, masks, and boots when handling contaminated materials.

Hurricane/Cyclone Safety Guide

Hurricanes can cause catastrophic damage. Preparation weeks in advance is crucial.

BEFORE HURRICANE SEASON (Preparation Phase)

Reinforce Your Home: Install storm shutters, strengthen garage doors, trim tree branches.
Board Up Windows: Have plywood or storm shutters ready before season starts.
Stock Supplies: Buy emergency kit items 6-8 weeks before hurricane season.
Prepare Transportation: Know evacuation routes, fill car with gas, keep vehicle serviced.

WHEN HURRICANE WATCH/WARNING ISSUED

WATCH: Conditions may occur | WARNING: Conditions expected - EVACUATE IF ORDERED
  • Fill bathtub with water for non-drinking use
  • Charge all phones, tablets, and power banks
  • Secure outdoor items or bring them inside
  • Close storm shutters or board up windows
  • Fill vehicle fuel tanks
  • Withdraw cash from ATM (cards may not work)
  • Prepare medications and medical equipment
  • Leave immediately if evacuation order issued

DURING THE HURRICANE

STAY INSIDE: Remain indoors in a small, interior room away from windows (bathroom, hallway, closet).
  • Do not go outside during the eye of the hurricane
  • Keep away from windows and glass doors
  • Do not use candles - use flashlights instead (fire hazard)
  • Keep your hurricane kit with you
  • Monitor local news and emergency broadcasts
  • Stay on the phone with family if possible

AFTER THE HURRICANE

Look Out For Hazards: Contaminated floodwater, gas leaks, downed power lines, debris.
  • Use generators and grills only outdoors (carbon monoxide danger)
  • Clear debris carefully - may contain sharp or hazardous materials
  • Avoid standing water
  • Do not touch downed utility lines
  • Document all damage with photos for insurance
  • Listen to local authorities for all-clear signal

Wildfire Safety Guide

Wildfires spread rapidly. Early warning and swift action are essential.

BEFORE WILDFIRE SEASON

Defensible Space: Clear leaves/branches from gutters, trim tree branches 10 feet from roof, remove dead trees.
Harden Your Home: Use fire-resistant roofing and siding, install metal vents, remove wooden fencing.
Know Your Routes: Identify at least two evacuation routes from your area.
Get N95 Masks: Stock masks for all family members for air quality issues.

DURING FIRE SEASON - IF WILDFIRE APPROACHING

Evacuate immediately if ordered - do not wait!
  • Prepare "Go Bag" with photos, documents, passport, medications
  • Pack vehicle the night before fire season
  • Keep phone charged and gas tank at least half full
  • Monitor air quality index for smoke
  • Wear N95 mask if air quality is poor
  • Close all windows and doors
  • Turn on car radio to hear evacuation alerts
  • Leave earlier rather than later - traffic may be heavy

EVACUATION STEPS

If You Stay: Close windows/doors, wet down house, turn on outside lights, park vehicle facing out.
If You Evacuate: Go to shelter, designated center, or stay with family. Leave NOW if ordered.

AFTER WILDFIRE

  • Check home only when authorities declare area safe
  • Watch for hotspots and re-ignition
  • Document all damage with photos
  • Air out home - do not run AC if smoke present
  • Clean or replace HVAC filters
  • Use air purifiers indoors

Tornado Safety Guide

Tornadoes can form quickly with little warning. Know where to shelter immediately.

BEFORE TORNADO SEASON

Identify Safe Rooms: Choose interior rooms on lowest floor away from windows (bathroom, basement, interior hallway).
Mobile Home Safety: Plan to evacuate to sturdy shelter - mobile homes offer no protection.
Family Drills: Practice tornado drills monthly during severe weather season.
Alert System: Get NOAA weather radio or alerts on your phone.

TORNADO WATCH vs. WARNING

WATCH: Conditions favorable - Be Ready | WARNING: Tornado Sighted - Take Shelter NOW!

IF TORNADO WARNING ISSUED

  • Go to safe room immediately
  • Close all windows and doors
  • Cover yourself with mattress, blankets, or helmet for protection
  • Crouch low and protect your head and neck
  • Stay away from windows, doors, outside walls
  • If in office/school - go to designated shelter area
  • If driving - do NOT try to outrun tornado
  • Pull over, get low in ditch, cover your head
  • If in open area - move away from tornado at right angle

AFTER TORNADO

Exit Safely: Check for injuries, hazards, gas leaks, and structural damage.
  • Do not go sightseeing - clear routes for emergency vehicles
  • Wear gloves, masks, and sturdy shoes when clearing debris
  • Do not touch downed power lines
  • Stay out of damaged buildings
  • Document damage with photos for insurance
  • Listen to local news for updates and recovery information
REMEMBER: In all emergencies, your life is more valuable than possessions. Evacuate when ordered, follow local authority instructions, and help others when safe to do so. This guide is for informational purposes - always follow official guidance from local emergency management authorities.
